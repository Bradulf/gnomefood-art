# Sporeling Evolution

This file tracks how the sporeling may change as the world evolves over time while preserving identity.

## Desert Wasteland Phase

The sporeling is dry, dust-covered, practical, and survival-oriented.

Visual traits:

- Faded wraps and patched field clothing.
- Minimal bioluminescence.
- Small water canister, seed pouch, or repaired tool.
- Cap may be cracked, sun-bleached, or shaded with cloth.
- Mycelium details are subtle and protective.

## Transitional Oasis Phase

The sporeling begins to show more active fungal symbiosis.

Visual traits:

- Slightly richer color and healthier fungal texture.
- Moisture tools, sprouting seed trays, or irrigation charms.
- More visible hyphae connecting clothing and tools.
- Small glowing spore details.

## Mycelial Settlement Phase

The sporeling becomes part of a thriving fungal agrarian community.

Visual traits:

- Woven mycelium garments and cultivated accessories.
- More confident posture.
- Tools integrated with bioelectric fungal systems.
- Visible community craft patterns.

## Magical Fungal World Phase

The sporeling may become luminous, ritual, and deeply connected to the living world.

Visual traits:

- Bioluminescent markings and spore aura.
- Ritual farming tools.
- Fungal growth that feels intentional and symbiotic.
- Still recognizable by cap silhouette, small scale, field-worker posture, and core accessories.

## Evolution Rule

Do not redesign the sporeling into a different character. Evolution should add materials, health, glow, and cultural detail while preserving the same identity anchors.
