# Sporeling Visual Rules

## Silhouette

The sporeling should be recognizable first by silhouette: small body, broad mushroom-cap head shape, practical carried tools, and a compact field-worker stance.

## Clothing

Use handmade agrarian survival clothing:

- Patched wraps, aprons, tunics, sashes, or shade cloth.
- Dust-stained fabrics in early desert scenes.
- Utility belts, seed pouches, hand tools, and moisture containers.
- Later scenes may add woven mycelium, bioluminescent thread, fungal lacquer, or ritual embroidery.

## Cyberpunk Details

Cyberpunk elements should feel salvaged and agricultural:

- Tiny sensor charms.
- Cable-wrapped tools.
- Solar battery packs.
- Repaired goggles or lenses.
- Bioelectric nodes grown into equipment.

Avoid sleek armor, generic neon streetwear, and clean futuristic styling.

## Fungal Details

Fungal traits should feel biological and practical:

- Cap texture, gills, spores, hyphae, rootlike fibers, and fungal skin patterns.
- Mycelium details may connect to tools, soil, or clothing.
- Bioluminescence should be restrained in early desert scenes and stronger in later magical phases.

## Consistency

Across images, preserve the same general proportions, cap silhouette, practical field-worker identity, and core accessories unless a scene-specific change is documented.
