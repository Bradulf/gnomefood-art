# Sporeling Poses

Use this file as a pose vocabulary when placing the sporeling into a specific parallax layer.

## Working Poses

- Kneeling beside a crop bed, inspecting soil or mycelium.
- Carrying a small seed sack, compost basket, or water vessel.
- Pulling or repairing an irrigation hose.
- Holding a salvaged farming tool with cable-wrapped handles.
- Planting spores or seeds into cracked ground.

## Traveling Poses

- Walking into wind with cloak or wraps trailing.
- Climbing over broken agricultural machinery.
- Crossing a dry channel or fungal bridge.
- Balancing a tool bundle over one shoulder.

## Ritual Poses

- Holding glowing spores in cupped hands.
- Marking a tool, stone, or pipe with fungal glyphs.
- Standing before a seed vault, shrine, pump, or mycelium node.
- Listening to the ground through rootlike fibers.

## Scale and Layer Notes

For distant layers, the sporeling should usually be a tiny silhouette or not appear at all.

For middle layers, the sporeling can show readable pose and context.

For artifact layers, the sporeling should appear only if interacting directly with the artifact.

For near layers, use partial close foreground silhouettes sparingly so the character identity is not distorted.
