# World Bible

## Core Premise

Gnomefood is set in a far-future post-apocalyptic world where a fungal, cyberpunk, agrarian civilization grows out of collapse. The story begins in a desert wasteland and gradually transforms into a magical fungal world filled with cultivated mycelium, bioelectric infrastructure, ritual agriculture, and strange communal machines.

## First World Phase: Desert Wasteland

The opening phase should feel dry, exposed, sun-struck, and exhausted. It is a place of sand-choked machinery, abandoned irrigation systems, cracked solar farms, rusted towers, buried settlements, and early fungal survival colonies clinging to shade and moisture.

Use this phase for scenes that show:

- Harsh desert horizons.
- Dead or repurposed industrial agriculture.
- Scavenged cyberpunk technology.
- Sparse fungal growth surviving in protected pockets.
- Early signs of magic emerging from decay.

## Civilization

The civilization is:

- Fungal: built around mushrooms, mycelium networks, spores, decomposition, symbiosis, and living architecture.
- Cyberpunk: improvised electronics, neon signals, hacked infrastructure, solar batteries, cables, antennas, sensors, and low-tech/high-tech hybrids.
- Agrarian: seed banks, terraces, irrigation, hand tools, compost, greenhouses, market gardens, pollination rituals, and food cultivation.
- Magical: bioluminescent spores, ritual glyphs, impossible growth, dreamlike weather, and ancient fungal intelligence.
- Post-apocalyptic: scarcity, salvage, adaptation, ruin, survival, and regeneration.

## Visual Evolution

The world should evolve over the scroll narrative:

1. Desert wasteland: dry ruin, sparse fungal signs, survival agriculture.
2. Transitional oasis: water returns, fungal structures spread, machines become symbiotic.
3. Mycelial settlements: cultivated mushroom architecture, communal farms, cybernetic irrigation.
4. Magical fungal world: dense luminous ecology, ritual technology, living cities, abundance after collapse.

## Recurring Motifs

- Mycelium as infrastructure.
- Mushrooms as homes, towers, umbrellas, machines, and crops.
- Desert technology overtaken by cultivated fungi.
- Agrarian tools upgraded with salvaged cybernetics.
- Tiny workers, farmers, engineers, and ritual figures implied through scale, not necessarily featured in every layer.
- Food as survival, culture, trade, and magic.

## Continuity Notes

Keep the world tactile and specific. Even when the story becomes magical, it should still feel rooted in farming, repair, water, soil, hunger, compost, and community.
