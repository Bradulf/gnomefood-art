# Global Style Guide

Use this file for art direction that should remain consistent across the whole gnomefood.com scroll experience. Scene-specific changes belong in each scene's `scene-style.md`.

## Overall Direction

Create cinematic, richly detailed, hand-crafted fantasy science fiction with fungal cyberpunk agrarian worldbuilding. The images should feel layered, tactile, and suitable for a vertical scroll parallax website using side-view environmental strips.

The visual language is environmental side-view terrain slices, not complete scenes, standalone illustrations, landscape paintings, concept art posters, centered hero shots, or detached dioramas on white or blank backgrounds.

## Asset Requirements

All generated layer assets should be:

- Full-width transparent PNGs.
- Isolated compositing strips, not finished backdrop composites.
- Side-view terrain slices for a parallax engine.
- Designed to work over other layers without a hard rectangular background.
- Horizontally expansive, with visual interest across the full width.
- Aligned to the shared top-to-bottom scroll route when the strip contains terrain.
- Cleanly cut out on transparency.
- Free of text, logos, UI, watermarks, borders, and frames.

## Composition Rules

Prompts should describe only the target layer. Do not ask the model to render a complete scene with all depth layers flattened together.

## Terrain Perspective Rules

- Terrain is viewed from the side.
- Terrain should support a top-to-bottom scroll route without becoming top-down or map-like.
- Layers are slices of a larger world.
- Layers are assembled later by the engine.
- Assets should not look like complete illustrations.
- Assets should not look like concept art posters.
- Assets should not look like aerial views.
- Assets should not look like top-down maps.
- Assets should not look like bird's-eye views.
- Assets should not look like centered hero shots.
- Assets should not look like detached dioramas sitting on white or blank backgrounds.

Use phrases such as:

- "isolated transparent PNG layer"
- "full-width parallax asset"
- "side-view terrain slice for a parallax engine"
- "the viewer moves down through the world as the page scrolls; the implied route enters near the top/north of the scene and exits near the bottom/south"
- "transparent background"
- "no sky fill outside the described silhouettes"
- "do not render a complete backdrop scene"
- "environmental slice, not a concept art poster"
- "terrain slice of a larger walkable world, not a detached diorama"

Avoid phrases such as:

- "full scene"
- "complete landscape"
- "finished backdrop"
- "poster"
- "illustration split into traditional depth perspective layers"
- "bird's-eye view"
- "aerial view"
- "top-down map"
- "hero shot"
- "detached diorama"

## Mood

The story should move from dry, harsh, damaged, and survival-focused toward lush, luminous, strange, communal, and magical.

Keep the tone mysterious and grounded. The world may become wondrous, but it should not become cute, generic, or polished fantasy.

## Materials

Common materials include:

- Sand, cracked clay, salt dust, rust, bone-dry wood.
- Mycelium, mushroom caps, spores, hyphae, compost, bark, roots.
- Salvaged metal, ceramic insulators, cables, solar glass, mesh, tubing.
- Irrigation channels, seed trays, sacks, tools, baskets, patched cloth.
- Bioluminescent gel, fungal lacquer, glowing glyphs, bioelectric nodes.

## Color Evolution

Early scenes should emphasize sun-faded ochre, rust, ash, bleached bone, dry cyan reflections, and sparse toxic neon.

Later scenes may introduce deeper greens, fungal whites, luminous blues, spore gold, wet soil browns, violet shadows, and magical bioluminescence.

Avoid making every scene the same palette. Let each scene's `scene-style.md` define its own color temperature, saturation, and magical intensity.
